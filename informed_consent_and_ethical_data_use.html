Informed Consent and Ethical Data Use

According to Cap. 486 Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance Section 35J:

  • The data user must inform the data subject in writing:
    • (i) That the data user intends to provide personal data.
    • (ii) That the data user may not so provide the data unless the data user has received the data subject's written consent to the intended provision.

Informed Consent and Ethical Data Use

According to Cap. 486 Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance Section 35J:

  • The data user must inform the data subject in writing:
    • (i) That the data user intends to provide personal data.
    • (ii) That the data user cannot provide data without written consent.
  • Provide the data subject with the following written details:
    • (i) If the data is for profit, explicitly mention it.
    • (ii) The types of personal data to be provided.
    • (iii) The categories of recipients for the data.
    • (iv) The categories of marketing subjects involved.
  • Ensure a method is available for the data subject to give consent without charge.
  • The business/company must inform the data provider of what the data will be used for.
  • Data should not be collected until consent is given for the intended purpose.
  • Provide written documents detailing:
    • Whether data will be used for profit or compensation.
    • What data would be provided for profit.
    • What kind of people or parties will receive the data.
    • What kind of marketing the data provider will be subjected to.
  • Provide a free method (e.g., contract signature) for the data provider to express consent.

Definitions

Prescribed Consent: Prescribed consent refers to the express consent given by a data subject for the intended use or provision of their personal data. This consent must be specific to the class of persons and the kind of personal data as specified in the information provided to the data subject. Thus, the consent given is specific to the data provider in this scenario.

Express Consent: Express consent refers to the clear, voluntary, and unequivocal agreement given by a person for the use or provision of their personal data. This type of consent is explicitly provided, rather than implied, and is necessary for certain data-processing activities.

Examples

Prescribed Consent Example: When a patient visits a hospital, they are often required to sign a consent form that allows the hospital to collect and use their personal health information for treatment purposes. This consent is specific to the healthcare providers involved and the type of data being collected, such as medical history and current health status.

Express Consent Example: When a user signs up for a social media platform, they are often required to agree to the platform's terms of service and privacy policy by checking a box or clicking "I agree." This action constitutes express consent, as the user is explicitly agreeing to the collection and use of their personal data as outlined in the terms and privacy policy. Unlike prescribed consent, which is specific to certain classes of persons and types of data, express consent in this context is a general agreement to the platform's data practices

Consent for Direct Marketing

According to Cap. 486 Section 35K:

  • A data user who has complied with section 35J must not provide the data subject's personal data to another person for the use by that other person in direct marketing unless-
    • (a) The data user has received the data subject's written consent to the intended provision of personal data, as described in the information provided by the data user under section 35(J) either generally or selectively.
    • (b) If the data is provided for gain, the intention to so provide was specified in the information under section 35(J)
    • (c) The provision is consistent with the data subject's consent

Definition of Direct Marketing

  • The offering, or advertising of the availability, of goods, facilities, or services through direct marketing means
  • The solicitation of donations and contributions for charitable, cultural, philanthropic, recreational, and political purposes through direct marketing means

Definition of Direct Marketing Means

  • Sending information or goods, addressed to specific persons by name, via mail, fax, electronic mail, or other means of communication.
  • Making telephone calls to specific persons.

Example

An NGO uses donors' contact details for a new fundraising campaign. Before doing so, they must obtain written consent, explicitly informing the donors of the intended use and providing opt-out options.

Consent for a New Purpose

According to Cap. 486 Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance Schedule 1Section 3 Principle 3 states that:

  • (1) Personal data shall not, without the prescribed consent of the data subject, be used for a new purpose
  • (2) A relevant person in relation to a data subject may, on his or her behalf, give the prescribed consent required for using his or her personal data for a new purpose if -
    • (a) The data subject is-
      • (i) a minor,
      • (ii) incapable of managing his or her own affairs; or
      • (iii) mentally incapacitated within the meaning of section2 of the Mental Health Ordinance
    • (b) The data subject is incapable of understanding the new purpose and deciding whether to give the prescribed consent

Example

An NGO that initially collected personal data from volunteers to coordinate event participation. Later, the NGO decides to use this data to send out invitations for a new training program. Since this is a different use than originally intended, the NGO must obtain new consent from the volunteers to use their data for this purpose. This ensures that the volunteers are aware of and agree to their data being used in a new way.
